Tidbits: On Taxes

February 13, 2017

I recently saw someone say that taxes are, or are at least like, theft. I’ve been thinking about this for a little while, now. It bothered me.

I’m not going to presume to know why folks hold this view. I’ll say that it just doesn’t make sense to me. Here’s why.

Taxes are payment for services rendered. They’re what we all pay so that we can live in a society. And, when I say “what we all pay”, I mean those of us who can afford to pay. Obviously, if you’re poor, how are you supposed to?

Which leads to another problem people seem to have with taxes. “Why should my taxes pay for your bills?” This is also very silly. Your taxes should pay for the bills of those who can’t afford theirs because other people’s taxes paid for the bills you never had to pay.

  • Were you born in a hospital? Did you build it?
  • Have you driven on a road recently? Did you build it?
  • Did you go to school? Did you build it?
  • Did you learn things at school? Did you teach yourself those things? Did you pay the teachers who taught you?

That’s the benefit of living in a society. When times are hard, there are people around, there are resources available, so that you don’t have to starve to death.

It’s unfortunate that these resources aren’t always used the way they should be. Yes, a lot of tax money is wasted by people in government. But whose fault is that? In a way, isn’t it ours for electing bad officials?

And that’s about where my spoons run out.
